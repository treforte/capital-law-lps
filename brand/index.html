Frequently Asked Questions

Our team has experience on both sides of the table — including defense work for insurance companies — which gives us insight into how carriers evaluate and try to minimize claims. We use that knowledge to fight for the maximum recovery on your behalf, and we never charge a fee unless we win.

A personal injury attorney investigates your accident, gathers evidence, communicates with insurance companies on your behalf, calculates the true value of your claim, and negotiates or litigates to recover compensation for your medical bills, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

We handle car, truck, motorcycle, rideshare, and bicycle accidents, pedestrian collisions, slip and fall and other premises liability cases, dog bites, catastrophic injuries, wrongful death, and claims against government entities.

If you were injured and someone else's negligence played a role — a careless driver, an unsafe property, a defective product — you likely have grounds for a claim. The best way to know for certain is a free, no-obligation consultation with our team.

Nothing upfront. We work on a contingency fee basis, meaning our fee is a percentage of what we recover for you. If we don't win your case, you owe us nothing. There's zero financial risk to finding out what your case may be worth.

Every case is different. Value depends on your injuries, medical costs, lost income, long-term care needs, pain and suffering, and who was at fault. California follows a pure comparative negligence rule, so you may still recover damages even if you were partially at fault. Call us for a free, honest assessment.

We recommend against giving a recorded statement or accepting any settlement offer before speaking with an attorney. Insurance adjusters work for the other side, and anything you say can be used to reduce your claim. Let our team handle all communication on your behalf.

Seek medical attention first, then document the scene if you're able to — photos, witness information, and a police or incident report. Avoid giving statements to insurance adjusters, and contact an attorney as soon as possible. In California, most personal injury claims must be filed within two years, and claims against government entities can have deadlines as short as six months, so time matters.
